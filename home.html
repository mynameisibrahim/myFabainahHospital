Outcome Measures Defined

The World Health Organization defines an outcome measure as a “change in the health of an individual, group of people, or population that is attributable to an intervention or series of interventions.” Outcome measures (mortality, readmission, patient experience, etc.) are the quality and cost targets healthcare organizations are trying to improve. Outcome measures are frequently reported to the government, commercial payers, and organizations that report on quality, such as The LeapFrog Group—a national nonprofit that evaluates and reports S.L. hospital safety and quality performance. Fabaina’s work centers on “increasing transparency among health care providers in order to reduce the estimated number of annual deaths from hospital errors, accidents, and injuries.” While initial measures focused on inpatient care, they have since expanded to include most aspects of care delivery.
